## 2. Who We Are and What We Do

The Reentry to Recovery Content Library is a free educational platform operated by a non-profit organization. It is designed to provide incarcerated and returning individuals in the United States with access to curated resources covering reentry, recovery, mental health, education, employment, parenting, legal information, and related topics.

The Platform is delivered through tablets and devices provided within participating correctional facilities. Access to the Platform is made available through your facility as a voluntary educational service.

## 4. Creating an Account

To access personalized features, you must create an account. When registering, you will provide:

- **Username** — A unique identifier you choose (letters, numbers, and underscores only)
- **First and last name**
- **Facility** — The correctional facility you are associated with
- **Inmate PIN** — A numeric PIN assigned through your facility's access process
- **Password** — Kept confidential and never stored in plain text
- **Security questions and answers** — Used only to verify your identity if you need to reset your password

You are responsible for keeping your password and security question answers confidential. Because devices may be shared, you should sign out of your account when you are finished using the Platform.

Your inmate PIN is used to verify your identity and connect your account to your facility. One account may be registered per inmate PIN at each facility.

## 5. Information We Collect and How We Use It

By using the Platform, we collect the following information:

**Account information**
Username, name, facility assignment, inmate PIN, and hashed (encrypted) security question answers. Passwords are never stored in readable form.

**Content engagement**
We track which content items you open, how long you spend actively engaging with each item, your progress through video and audio content (e.g., how far through a video you watched), whether you complete items, and the date items are completed. This data is used to display your personal progress on your dashboard and to generate anonymized program reports for the facility and the non-profit.

**Ratings**
If you rate content as "Helpful" or "Not Helpful," your rating is recorded and used to improve content curation. Facility-level and category-level reports show only aggregate totals (e.g., how many users rated an item helpful) without identifying who gave each rating. However, individual user progress reports — accessible to facility administrators and our non-profit staff — do show which specific items you rated and how you rated them.

**Bookmarks**
If you save resources using the bookmark feature, those saved items are stored and associated with your account so you can find them again.

**Login history**
The dates you log in are recorded to calculate engagement statistics such as your day streak. Individual login records are not shared externally.

**Analytics events**
General usage events — such as which categories you visit — are collected in aggregate to help us understand how the Platform is being used across all facilities.

**What we do not collect**
We do not collect payment information, location data, biometric data, or any information from third-party advertising or tracking services. The Platform does not use third-party advertising.

**How we use your information**
- To provide and display your personal progress dashboard
- To calculate engagement tiers and facility rankings (anonymized)
- To generate program reports for facility administrators and grant reporting
- To improve content quality and curation based on aggregate usage patterns
- To allow you to reset your password using your security questions

We do not sell your personal information to any third party.
